FACTORS THAT DEFINE DIAMOND VALUE

Carat weight: The weight of the diamond is a significant factor. Generally, as the carat weight increases, so does the price, assuming other factors are equal.
Cut Quality: The cut quality affects the diamond's brilliance and how well it reflects light. Diamonds with better cuts are more desirable and thus more valuable.
Color Grade: Diamonds are graded on a scale from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow or brown). Colorless diamonds (D-F) are rarer and usually more valuable.
Clarity Grade: This refers to the presence of internal or external flaws (inclusions and blemishes). Diamonds with fewer flaws are more valuable.
Shape: Certain shapes may be more popular or desirable, influencing their price. Round diamonds typically command higher prices due to their popularity and brilliance.
Market trends: Supply and demand dynamics, economic conditions, and trends in jewelry fashion can all impact diamond prices.
